Cosmology and the Structure of the Universe

Cosmology is the scientific study of the origin, evolution, and eventual fate of the universe. It combines insights from astronomy, physics, and mathematics to explore the cosmos as a coherent whole.

Large-Scale Structure

The universe exhibits a complex web-like structure on the largest scales, composed of galaxies, galaxy clusters, filaments, and vast voids. These structures emerged from initial density fluctuations after the Big Bang.

Dark Matter and Dark Energy

Observational evidence suggests that ordinary matter constitutes only about 5% of the universe. The rest is made up of dark matter, which exerts gravitational pull, and dark energy, which drives the accelerated expansion of the cosmos.

The Role of Gravity

Gravity shapes the universe at all scales. It governs the formation of stars, galaxies, and black holes, and may even hold clues to the quantum structure of spacetime itself.
